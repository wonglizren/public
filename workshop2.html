so what's buddhism got to do with it?

The machine runs on
three familiar fuels.

Craving — you must keep wanting. Dissatisfaction — you must never feel you have enough. Identity — you must believe stuff says who you are.

Sound familiar? Those are the exact three things the Buddha diagnosed 2,500 years before the first shopping mall. Materialism didn't invent the thirst — the machine industrialised it.

the diagnosis · taṇhā

Craving is the cause

Taṇhā — literally thirst. The grasping that whispers "this isn't enough." Feed it, and like fire it doesn't get satisfied — it gets bigger.

inside the machine · open each stage

How it keeps you thirsty.

⚙️It stimulates cravingstage 1

Advertising works by first creating a sense of lack — a "need" you didn't have five seconds ago. It manufactures the thirst, then charges you for water.

⚙️It strengthens the clinging of selfstage 2

Every purchase becomes a brick in "who I am". The more me is built out of stuff, the more there is to defend, compare, and lose.

⚙️It keeps the mind searching outwardstage 3

Happiness is always out there — next drop, next upgrade, next haul. A mind aimed permanently outward never gets to settle where it actually lives.

⚙️It depends on impermanence — but denies itstage 4

The whole model needs things to fade — trends die, tech ages, joy wears off — or you'd stop buying. Yet every ad promises this one will last. It runs on anicca while selling you its opposite.

⚙️It dulls gratitude and contentmentstage 5

Contentment is the one thing the machine can't sell — so it's treated as the enemy. "Enough" gets drowned out by "next". Gratitude is the off-switch.

the kilesas · defilements of the mind · tap each

The three poisons
under the hood.

Kilesa = any defilement — the mind's weeds. The traditional lists run long: greed, hatred, delusion, conceit, wrong view, doubt, restlessness, shamelessness…

The three poisons are the three root kilesas — the akusala-mūla, "unwholesome roots" — that every other one grows from. So: every poison is a kilesa; these three are the roots of the whole tangle. (In Theravāda they're often just called the three unwholesome roots; "three poisons" is the catchy borrowed name.)

all kilesas⊃lobha · dosa · moha (the roots)
🤑Lobhagreed
😤Dosahatred / aversion
🌫️Mohadelusion
Tap a poison.

Kilesas are the mind's pollutants — the states that cloud, burn, and drive it. Three are called the roots of all the rest.

And beneath even greed and hatred sits moha — delusion, ignorance: not-seeing things as they are. That's the taproot of all defilements. The machine doesn't need you evil — it just needs you not looking closely. Which is why seeing clearly is the whole practice.
so what do we do — quit the world?

No. Just stop being
a buyer on autopilot.

You'll still buy things. Autopilot is just moha wearing a wallet — the move is to see the machine while it works on you: notice the manufactured lack, name the flavour of thirst, remember the last "must-have" that's now in a drawer.

Every time you see the mechanism, moha thins. And a mind that sees clearly can enjoy things without being owned by them.
